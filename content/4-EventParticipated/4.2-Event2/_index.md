## FCJ Community Day - May 23, 2026

**Role:** Attendee

## Focus areas

- AI engineering in production.
- Skills needed to stay relevant as AI tools improve.
- Business expectations for AI systems.
- Security, compliance, context engineering, and agent system design.
- Cloud cost control and edge/security topics.

## Key takeaways

One major theme was that AI raises the minimum acceptable level of performance, so engineers need to use AI proactively rather than passively depend on it.

Speakers emphasized that AI projects should start from concrete business or technical use cases instead of vague experimentation.

For enterprise environments, AI systems require strong attention to security, compliance, guardrails, and permission boundaries.

Context engineering was presented as highly important: AI systems perform better when they are given clear goals, correct context, and appropriate boundaries.

There was also discussion of MCP integration, observability for agent systems, and the risks of prompt injection or unsafe tool use.

On the infrastructure side, cost and abuse prevention were highlighted, including traffic spikes, DDoS-style concerns, and the value of managed edge/network services.

## Career relevance

This event reinforced that building AI in production is not just about prompting or model choice.

It influenced how I framed my workshop around validation, fallback handling, observability, and cleanup, rather than only the happy-path demo.
