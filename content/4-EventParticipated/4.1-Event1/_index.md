## FCJ Community Day - March 21, 2026

**Role:** Attendee

## Focus areas

- Platform Engineering, DevOps, cloud architecture, and AI/LLM operations.
- Differences and overlap between Platform Engineer and DevOps Engineer.
- Microservices, Django, Go, and self-service platform thinking.
- Infrastructure as Code, observability, automation, and reusable internal platforms.

## Key takeaways

A recurring idea was moving from `Platform as a Service` to `Platform as a Product`: the platform should be reusable, secure, and easy for internal users to consume.

Platform work is not limited to AWS. It requires understanding multiple tools and, more importantly, how to connect them into a usable workflow.

Self-service matters: downstream users such as developers or data engineers should be able to move faster without needing constant platform support.

Infrastructure as Code, monitoring, auditing, centralization, and consolidation were emphasized as core platform capabilities.

In AI systems, observability is critical. Relevant tools and ideas mentioned included `Langfuse`, `OpenTelemetry`, model selection, deployment, monitoring, and iterative model adaptation.

There was also discussion of practical tradeoffs such as serverless inference, cold start concerns, and the importance of balancing speed with quality.

## Career relevance

I learned that platform engineering is as much about product thinking and internal user experience as it is about infrastructure.

This helped me think more clearly about the observability and operational evidence parts of my workshop, not just the RAG pipeline itself.
