## Saturday Meet-up - June 6, 2026

**Role:** Attendee

## Focus areas

- Career development in the AI/cloud market.
- Docker and containerization in real engineering work.
- GraphRAG and retrieval limitations.
- Operations, troubleshooting, and production mindset.
- Interview preparation and industry expectations.

## Key takeaways

A strong message from the event was that engineers should keep using AI as a tool while continuing to build skills that require judgment, initiative, and system thinking.

One speaker discussed Docker from a practical perspective: containers are useful not only for packaging applications but also for isolation, testing, and security-related workflows.

GraphRAG was mentioned as useful when relationships between entities matter and when plain semantic chunk retrieval is not enough.

Production operations examples highlighted the importance of monitoring, incident response, and troubleshooting under pressure.

Interview advice centered on understanding the target role, learning the technologies actually used in the market, and explaining troubleshooting and deployment thinking clearly.

Another important point was that end-to-end projects and labs help compensate for lack of formal work experience.

## Career relevance

This event supported my decision to keep my internship workshop practical and end-to-end instead of purely theoretical.

It also reinforced the value of documenting operational evidence, architecture decisions, and real deployment considerations in the final repo.
